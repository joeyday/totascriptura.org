## What the five solas were reacting against

- Sola Scriptura (“Scripture alone”) vs. Scripture + Tradition and Magisterium
- Sola fide (“faith alone”) vs. faith + works
- Sola gratia (“grace alone”) vs. grace + merit
- Solus Christus (“Christ alone”) vs. Christ + the Pope and priestly order
- Soli Deo gloria (“glory to God alone”) vs. glory to God + veneration of Mary, saints, and angels

## More *sola fide* passages

I’ve pulled the support here for *sola fide* largely from chapter 9 of Matthew Barrett’s <cite>Faith Alone: The Doctrine of Justification</cite>. He continues to give yet more Scriptural support in chapters 10–15. Chapters 8 and 16 appear very helpful for understanding why good works cannot be effectual for justification (chapter 8 deals with human sin and chapter 16 deals with the role of good works).

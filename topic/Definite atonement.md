**Definite atonement** addresses the intent and scope of the atoning death of Christ. It maintains that Christ suffered for sin and secured redemption only for those elected by God to salvation. For a general overview of the atonement, see [[topic/Atonement|atonement]].

## Designed to secure salvation, not merely make it possible

### Secures salvation

Isa 19:20; 53:4–12; Mt 18:11; Jn 3:16–17; Ro 3:23–25; 5:8–10, 18–19; 8:3–4, 28–34; 1Co 6:19–20; 2Co 5:14–21; Gal 1:3–4; 3:13; 4:4–5; Eph 1:3, 7, 14; 2:14–16; 5:2, 25–27; Col 1:12–14; 2Ti 1:9–10; Tit 2:13–14; Heb 1:3; 2:10; 7:25; 9:11–15, 26; 10:14; 13:12; 1Pe 2:24; 3:18; 1Jn 1:7; 4:9; Rev 1:5; 5:9–10

### Also secures fulfillment of conditions for salvation
{{mt|[[topic/Effectual grace|Effectual grace]]}}
Gal 3:13–14; Eph 1:3–4; 2:4–8; Php 1:29

## Limited in scope to the elect

### Christ was sent for the elect

Jn 17:19 ~(cf. Jn 10:36)~; Heb 2:14–17

### Christ died for the elect

Isa 53:8, 11–12; Mt 1:21; 20:28; 26:28; Lk 1:68; Jn 6:38–39; 10:11, 14–15; 11:51–52; 13:1; 15:13; 17:1–2; Ac 20:28; Ro 5:19; 8:28, 33–34; Gal 1:4; 2:20; Eph 5:2, 25–27; Tit 2:14; Heb 9:15, 28; 13:20; 1Jn 3:16; 4:10; Rev 5:9–10

### Christ intercedes for the elect

{{[[intercession]]}}

### Propitiation and intercession inseparably linked

Isa 53:11–12; Ro 8:32–34; Heb 7:24–27; 9:11–12; 10:19–22; 1Jn 2:1–2

## Objections

### Christ died for “the world”

Jn 1:29; 3:17; 4:42; 6:33, 51; Ro 11:12, 15; 2Co 5:19; 1Jn 2:2 ~(contrast Jn 11:51–52)~; 1Jn 4:14

### Christ died for “all” or “everyone”

Ro 5:18; 1Co 15:22; 2Co 5:14–15; 1Ti 2:5–6; 4:10; Tit 2:11; Heb 2:9

### God desires salvation of all

1Ti 2:4; 2Pe 3:9

### Some for whom Christ died may not ultimately be saved

Ac 20:28–30; Ro 14:15; 1Co 8:11; Heb 10:29; 2Pe 2:1

## Counter-objections

### “World” doesn't always mean everyone in the world

Lk 2:1; Jn 1:10; 7:4; 12:19; 14:22; 18:20; Ac 11:28; 19:27; 24:5; Ro 1:8; 11:12, 15; Col 1:6

### “World” in some places carries idea of “all nations”

Mt 24:14; 26:13; Mk 16:15; Ro 1:5; 10:18

### “All” or “every” ~(gr. *pas*)~ can be distributive ~(i.e., “every kind”)~

Mt 4:23 ~(also Mk 9:35; 10:1)~; Lk 11:42
